# About Santiago

**Santiago Vivas-Gonzalez** is a Product Manager who builds, with 5+ years specializing in AI/ML/LLM, 0→1, and multi-tenant SaaS. He's based in Chicago and currently works at Capital One on AI/ML platform analytics, strategy, and operations while building Chag Innovations LLC on the side.

## Background

Santiago has shipped product across enterprise AI/ML platforms (Capital One), SaaS data visualization (Golden Software), MedTech (Stryker), and 0-to-1 startups (Chag Innovations, Bumpin).

At Capital One, he built the analytics function from zero for an org serving 12,000+ data scientists and ML engineers across 7 multi-tenant platform towers, surfacing 73 zombie features (23.7% operational compute waste) and ~$247K/yr in recurring compute savings. He then led the enterprise rollout of AI-powered development tools to 16,000+ associates at 80% adoption in one month.

At Golden Software, he diagnosed first-session churn and shipped Templater, an adaptive template gallery that doubled monthly active users from 30K to 62K and lifted Grapher's SUS from 55 to 75 and NPS from 45 to a peak of 77 across four coordinated UX-redesign releases. He installed Continuous Discovery Habits as the operating system inside Jira Product Discovery and stood up the company's first usability study.

At Stryker, he owned the user-facing components of the next-generation Mako Surgical Robot, running usability testing with 50+ surgeons and OR staff and building a Visual Basic field-complaint pipeline that lifted investigation efficiency 200% and became the post-market surveillance intake standard for the division.

He holds a Master of Product Design & Development Management from Northwestern and a BS in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Maryland. He is IBM AI Product Manager certified and Nielsen Norman Group UX certified (NN/g).

## Why Chag Innovations

Santiago founded Chag Innovations because he wanted to build, not just spec. Most product managers hand off requirements and wait. Santiago writes production React and Python, designs the UX, architects the AI pipelines, runs his own sprints, deploys to production, and responds to user feedback, all as a solo founder.

Chaggerheart is the result: a live product with 65% first-session character creation, 95% of post-launch organic acquisition arriving after the AI-rebrand, 1,990 automated tests across 44 suites, and a 5-agent LangGraph pipeline running session audio into a structured campaign journal at zero infra cost through MVP.

The studio's thesis is straightforward: small, underserved communities deserve well-built software, and a product manager who can also engineer is the fastest path to giving it to them.
